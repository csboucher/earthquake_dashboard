A Century of Earthquakes in The East African Rift Zone

Hypothesis: The earthquakes in the East African Rift System are shallow (less than 25km) and have seismic variations due to the volcanism in the area.
There are many earthquakes that are at a 33km depth which is slightly deeper than expected but is still reasonable for the shallow lithosphere in the rift. There are a few approximately 5.0 magnitude outliers that happened at a depth greater than 33km depth. The seismicity does vary but this could also be from an increase in seismometers deployed.
